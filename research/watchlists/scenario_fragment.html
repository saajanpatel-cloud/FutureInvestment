Forward-multiple scenario model

What is this? A quantitative bull / base / bear framework for each proposed name. For every ticker: take today's trailing earnings (or revenue per share for pre-profit names), grow them at three rates over 3 years, apply an exit multiple, and compare the implied price to today's price. The weighted column blends all three scenarios by probability. Edit assumptions in scenario_assumptions.csv and re-run the script.

Generated 2026-05-17T15:46:07Z. Not predictions — illustrative scenarios to frame your own research. Verify in filings.

How to read this: CAGR = compound annual growth rate over 3 years. Exit = the P/E (or P/S) multiple you think the market will assign at the end of year 3. Implied = the share price those inputs produce. Δ = percentage change from today's price. Weighted = probability-blended target across all three scenarios.

Methodology notes
  • Metric: Forward EPS (analyst consensus NTM) from yfinance where available, trailing EPS as fallback. Revenue per share for pre-profit names (IONQ). Forward EPS avoids distortions from stock comp, one-offs, or cyclical troughs.
  • Projection: metric × (1 + CAGR)³ × exit multiple = implied price.
  • Weighted target: Σ(scenario price × probability). Default split is 25/50/25 bull/base/bear; IONQ uses 20/45/35 given higher uncertainty.
  • Limitations: Single-metric model; ignores buybacks, dilution, dividends, and balance-sheet changes. Meant for relative comparison across the watchlist, not absolute price targets.
  • Edit: Change assumptions in research/watchlists/scenario_assumptions.csv and re-run python scripts/fi_scenarios.py.
